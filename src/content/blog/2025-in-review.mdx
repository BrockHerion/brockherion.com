Coming off the heels of an amazing trip to Jamaica, I had very high hopes going into 2025. While the year did not quite go as I expected it to, there's still a lot of really amazing things that happened that are worth talking about. This year had a lot of moments of learning and realization. It let me really reflect on my life and where I wanted to go.

With that, here's 2025 in review.

## Focus on mental health

A big part of 2025 for me was focusing on my mental health. After a few rough patches in 2024, I made the choice to see a psychiatrist and get medicated for my anxiety. Anxiety has long been a part of my life, impacting everything from my performance in school to how I competed in gymnastics. For awhile, I used it to my advantage. It drove me, pushed me forward, kept driving me to step outside of my comfort zone. That drive came at a high cost though. It kept me in a constant state of fight-or-flight, and I could never truly relax. And coupled with perfectionist and people pleasing tendencies, it lead to a state of very high stress that I could not escape from.

Getting medicated and going back to therapy were great steps towards combating this. While it took awhile to find medication that worked for me, I can safely say my anxiety is now under control. It took me awhile to learn how to not operate on pure stress and anxiety, but I feel much freer now. Things that used to send me into high-anxiety no longer do. I feel much more able to approach things would have otherwise stressed me out to the point of avoidance.

There's still work to be done of course, but this portion of my journey has been well worth it. I feel good being able to focus on things that matter in my life, and be present for the good and bad moments. It's allowed me to slow down and take things in. Overall, I'm very happy I decided to take control of my anxiety and get help for it.

## Realigning my commitments

One benefit/curse of this was mental health shift was being able to say no to things. If something was going to take time away from my other goals, it became a no. This was liberating, as I have been able to accomplish a lot, and also take relaxing evenings for myself. The downside here is that I have start to over-commit to things as well. With my new found ability to say no, I felt I could take more on. This double-edged sword caused me a lot of grief throughout the course of the year. I picked up more projects, coaching, home improvement, and a lot more. That's all well and good, but I started replacing my free time with more work instead of taking time for myself.

I enjoy reading, writing, playing video games, watching movies, lifting weights. All these things started to fall off as I put more and more on my plate. I left no time for me. This is something that I'm still working through and starting to find a better balance with. Self-care has a been a large topic of conversation in therapy. Being more mindful about taking time for me has been great. Whether it's going to the gym or reading a book or picking up a new game, I'm trying to be more aware of when I need to have "me time" and step away from the hustle and bustle of work.
